Counter vs Healthy Choice vs Lean Cuisine: I Compared the Nutrition Data

Three brands, three different strategies for "high protein" frozen meals. I pulled the labels and ran the numbers side by side.

If you have searched for a high-protein frozen meal recently, you have probably narrowed it down to one of these three: Counter, Healthy Choice Power Bowls, or Lean Cuisine Protein Kick. They sit in the same freezer aisle, target the same shopper, and all put protein front and center on the box.

But when you compare the actual nutrition data, these are three very different products.

The Head-to-Head Numbers

Counter (6 products)
Protein: 30-31g | Calories: 310-370 | Ratio: 0.084-0.100 | Price: $5.99

Healthy Choice Power Bowls (12+ products)
Protein: 19-26g | Calories: 200-340 | Ratio: 0.070-0.095 | Price: $4.49-5.49

Lean Cuisine Protein Kick (8+ products)
Protein: 18-20g | Calories: 250-370 | Ratio: 0.054-0.080 | Price: $3.99-4.49

The protein gap is significant. Counter delivers 30-31g per meal as a floor, not a ceiling. Every single product hits that range. Healthy Choice tops out at 26g on their best SKU (Chicken Feta Bowl) but averages closer to 22g. Lean Cuisine Protein Kick sits at 18-20g across the board.

That means swapping a Lean Cuisine for a Counter meal nets you 11-13 extra grams of protein. Over three meals a day, that is 33-39g, roughly the equivalent of an entire chicken breast.

Where Each Brand Wins

Counter wins on protein and ratio. No other brand in this comparison delivers 30g+ consistently. The protein-to-calorie ratios (0.084-0.100) are the tightest range of any frozen meal brand I have tested. Their cottage cheese and Greek yogurt sauce base is what makes the math work. The protein comes from the food itself, not from additives.

Healthy Choice wins on availability and variety. With 30,000+ retail locations and 12+ SKUs in the Power Bowls line alone, you can find Healthy Choice almost anywhere. They also cover more cuisine types, from Korean beef to Mediterranean bowls. If variety matters, this is the biggest menu.

Lean Cuisine wins on price. At $3.99-4.49 per meal, Lean Cuisine Protein Kick is the cheapest option in this comparison. However, the cost per gram of protein ($0.22) is actually higher than Counter ($0.20) because you are getting less protein per box. The sticker price is lower, but the protein value is not.

The Ratio Tells the Real Story

Grams of protein on the box are only half the picture. A meal with 20g of protein and 370 calories (Lean Cuisine Sesame Chicken, ratio 0.054) is fundamentally different from a meal with 31g of protein and 310 calories (Counter Lazy Lasagna, ratio 0.100). The Counter meal delivers nearly twice the protein efficiency per calorie.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Counter vs Healthy Choice: which has more protein?
A: Counter has significantly more protein per meal. Counter delivers 30-31g of protein across all products, while Healthy Choice Power Bowls range from 19-26g depending on the SKU. Counter's lowest-protein meal (30g) still has 4g more than Healthy Choice's highest (26g in the Chicken Feta Bowl). Counter also wins on protein-to-calorie ratio, averaging 0.089 versus Healthy Choice's 0.078.

Q: Is Counter better than Lean Cuisine?
A: On protein, yes. Counter delivers 30-31g per meal versus Lean Cuisine Protein Kick's 18-20g. Counter also wins on protein-to-calorie ratio (0.084-0.100 vs 0.054-0.080) and cost per gram of protein ($0.20 vs $0.22). Lean Cuisine has a lower sticker price ($3.99-4.49 vs $5.99) and much wider availability (30,000+ stores vs 4,200+ stores). If pure protein is your priority, Counter is the better choice. If budget and convenience matter most, Lean Cuisine is easier to find and cheaper per box.

Q: What is the healthiest frozen meal brand?
A: "Healthiest" depends on your goals. For protein density (most protein per calorie), Counter leads with a 0.084-0.100 protein-to-calorie ratio across all products. For calorie control, Healthy Choice has some of the lowest-calorie options (Basil Pesto Chicken at 200 cal). For budget-friendly protein, Vital Pursuit offers 25-30g at $4.49-5.49.
